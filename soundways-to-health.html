The Science of Sound

Why vibration heals

Sound is not only heard — it is felt. When precisely tuned frequencies move through the body, the nervous system responds in measurable ways.

The relaxation response

Slow, resonant tones help shift the body out of "fight or flight" and into parasympathetic rest — the state in which repair happens.

Brainwave states

Steady rhythms and binaural beats are studied for their role in guiding the mind toward calmer theta and delta states associated with deep rest.

Vibration & the body

Vibroacoustic approaches apply therapeutic frequency directly to the body, inviting tension to release at a physical, cellular level.

Calm you can measure

Research links sound meditation with lower tension and improved mood — a felt shift that begins in a single session.

In an observational study of 62 participants, singing-bowl sound meditation was associated with significantly reduced tension, anger, fatigue and depressed mood — with the greatest drop in tension among first-time participants.

Goldsby et al., Journal of Evidence-Based Complementary & Alternative Medicine, 2017 (UC San Diego). Read the study →
